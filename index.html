Rasmus Fonseca

I study efficient computational methods for modeling macromolecules (proteins, RNA and DNA) and computations of geometric properties both in 2, 3, and higher dimensions.

I am currently a postdoc at the Department of Computer Science, University of Copenhagen (DIKU). I did my Ph.D. studies at DIKU in Prof. Pawel Winters lab and at University of California, Santa Cruz, in collaboration with Prof. Kevin Karplus. From 2012-2014 I was a postdoc at INRIA Saclay with Dr. Julie Bernauer and at SLAC Stanford with Dr. Henry van den Bedem. From Dec. 2015 I'll be at Stanford University again.

  • Various software projects I (co-)authored.
    • Flareplot - Interactive visualization tool for dynamic networks.
    • KGSrna - System for characterizing the conformational flexibility of RNA orders of magnitude faster than traditional MD methods.
    • High-dimensional Steiner trees - A C++ library for finding optimal Steiner trees in any dimension.
    • ProGAL - Protein Geometric Algorithm Library. A Java library with algorithms and data structures for analyzing and representing protein structures.
    • PDBCluster - Small C++ program that performs k-medoids and k-centers clustering of protein/RNA/DNA PDB-files using Partitioning Around Medoids (PAM) and the dRMSD metric.
    • KredsDesign - Visual hardware simulator for a machine architecture class. Suitable companion for the book "Computer Organization and Design".
  • Useful implementations - Solutions to small problems that I couldn't find implementations to anywhere else
    • Java-implementation finding the cartesian coordinates of a regular n-simplex
    • Java-implementation that solves the problem of Apollonius (i.e. finding a circle tangent to three other circles)
    • Problem of Apollonius in 3D (i.e. finding a sphere tangent to four other spheres. Step-by-step description easily generalized to d-dimensions)
